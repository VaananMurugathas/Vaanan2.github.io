Timeline of previous pandemics

By analyzing this data we can learn about next steps for what needs to be done in the current day

  • Covid-19: Infectious disease, transmitted through droplets generated by the infected, mild to moderate symptoms for most however, to many groups it is dangerous.
  • Sars: Sever acute respiratory syndrome. SARS was first reported in Asia however, quickly grew to become a global phenomenon. This occurred during 2003.
  • Spanish flu: Also referred to as the 1918 flu pandemic. It was a deadly influenza pandemic that lasted about 15 months. During this time it infiect 500 million people.
  • Yellow Fever: viral infection transmitted by a bite from infected mosquitoes often found in South America and Africa. The virus damages internal organs.
  • Cholera: There have been many cholera outbreaks however, the first was in India during 1817. Leads to extreme dehydration. Caused by eating/drinking contaminated substances.
  • The Black Death: Known as pestilence and the Great Mortality. Most fatal pandemic recorded in human history with over 75-200 million people dying in Eurasia and North Africa.
  • Smallpox: Serious infectious disease. It was extremely contagious. It was an airborne disease. Smallpox was a very serious illness and a majority of those who caught it died.
